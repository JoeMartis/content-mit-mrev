Basis Vectors and Vector decomposition

1. Unit Vectors and Basis Vectors

 

A unit vector is just any vector of magnitude 1. There is an especially useful set of unit vectors composed of three unit vectors in the directions of the +x, +y and +z axes in a Cartesian coordinate system. Together these are called basis vectors (advanced readers: an orthonormal basis). The most common way to denote these three vectors is to use the letters i, j, k with a circumflex or ''hat'' on top, instead of the usual arrow:    \(\hat{i},\; \hat{j},\; \hat{k}\) as shown in the following figure. Also common is using the letters x, y, z:  \(\hat{x},\; \hat{y},\; \hat{z}\). The hat always distinguishes a unit vector.
