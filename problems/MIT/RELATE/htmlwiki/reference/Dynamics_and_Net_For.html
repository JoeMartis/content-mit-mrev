Compatible Systems

This Model is only capable of describing the translational motion of a single point particle. Rigid bodies or multi-body systems can be described by this model only if they are treated as a point particle located at the system's center of mass.

Relevant Interactions

As described in Newton's Second Law, the acceleration of a system is determined by taking the net external force acting on the system and dividing by the system's mass. Thus, only external forces need be considered when finding the acceleration.

Definitions

Acceleration (Newton's Second Law)

$\vec{a} = \frac{1}{m} \sum_{ext} \vec{F}$

Mathematical Representation

Differential Form

$\frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = \vec{a}(t) $

$\frac{d\vec{r}}{dt} = \vec{v}(t) $

Integral Form

$\vec{v}_{f}=\vec{v}_{i}+\int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} \vec{a}(t) dt$

$\vec{r}_{f}=\vec{r}_{i}+\int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} \vec{v}(t) dt$
