Restrictions to the Mechanical Energy, External Work, and Internal Non-Conservative Work Model

Constant Mechanical Energy is a sub-model of the Mechanical Energy, External Work, and Non-Conservative Internal Work Model. This model applies in the case that the net external force applied to the system is zero and any internal non-conservative forces (such as kinetic friction) do no work. If only conservative external work is done, it is usually possible to apply the Constant Mechanical Energy Model by redefining the system to make that work internal. It can then be accounted for in the potential energy of the redefined system.

Mathematical Representation

$\mathcal{}E^{mech}_{f} = E^{mech}_{i}$
