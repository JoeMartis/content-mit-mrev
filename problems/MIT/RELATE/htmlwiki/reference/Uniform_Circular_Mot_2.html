Restrictions to the Velocity and Acceleration Model

Uniform Circular Motion is a sub-Model of the Velocity and Acceleration model. A point particle can be described by this Model if it is moving in a circular path with constant speed. Under these circumstances, the acceleration experienced by the point particle will necessarily have constant size and will always point toward the center of the circular path. In the equations that follow, it will be assumed that the origin of the coordinate system is chosen to coincide with the center of the circular path.

Definitions

Centripetal Acceleration

$\vec{a} = -\frac{v^{2}}{r} \hat{r}$

Phase

$\phi = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{y_{i}}{x_{i}}\right)$

Mathematical Representation

In this section, we further assume that the point particle is moving counterclockwise as seen by an observer looking down the z-axis. If the point particle is actually moving clockwise, simply replace v / r in the following equations with − v / r.

$x(t) = r \cos\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$

$y(t) = r \sin\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$

$v_{x}(t) = - v\sin\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$

$v_{y}(t) = v\cos\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$

$a_{x}(t) = -\frac{v^{2}}{r}\cos\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$

$a_{y}(t) = -\frac{v^{2}}{r}\sin\left(\frac{v}{r}t+\phi\right)$
