Illustrative Example: Eccentricity of One-Half.

Suppose a planet of mass m is in an elliptical orbit about a star of mass M, where M >> m. If the periapsis distance of the orbit is exactly a/2, what is the ratio of the kinetic energy in the system to the potential energy at the periapsis and at the apoapsis?

Solution [show]
System: [show system]
The planet is approximated as a point particle and the star as an infinitely massive object.

Interactions: [show interactions]
The gravitational interaction between the planet and star.

Models: [show Models]
Both the Keplerian Orbit Model and the Constant Mechanical Energy Model will be used.

Quantitative Approach: [show approach]

Total Mechanical Energy [show]
Since we have the masses of both objects and the semimajor axis, we know by the properties of the Keplerian Orbit Model that the total energy of the orbit is:

\(-\frac{GMm}{2a}\)

Apply Energy Conservation at Periapsis [show]
Since the periapsis distance is a/2, we know that the total energy at periapsis is:

\( E_{p} = K_{p} - U_{p} = K_{p} - \frac{GMm}{\frac{1}{2}a} \)

Since we know the total energy of the orbit, we can solve this for the kinetic energy:

\( K_{p} = E + U_{p} = -\frac{GMm}{2a} + \frac{GMm}{\frac{1}{2}a} = \frac{3GMm}{2a} \)

Thus, the ratio of kinetic energy to potential energy at periapsis is:

\( \frac{K_{p}}{|U_{p}|} = \frac{3GMm/2a}{2GMm/a} = \frac{3}{4}\)

which is larger than 1/2.

Apoapsis [show]
Remembering the relationship that the periapsis distance plus the apoapsis distance must equal the full major axis distance, we find the apoapsis distance to be:

\( r_{a} = 2a - r_{p} = 2a-\frac{1}{2}a = \frac{3}{2}a\)

Following the same procedure as we did with the periapsis energy, it is possible to show that the ratio of kinetic to potential energy at apoapsis is:

\( \frac{K_{a}}{|U_{a}|} = \frac{1}{4}\)

Understand the Answer [show]
The result of this problem is general in the sense that at periapsis the kinetic energy will be greater than 1/2 the size of the potential energy and at apoapsis the kinetic energy will be less than 1/2 the size of the potential.
