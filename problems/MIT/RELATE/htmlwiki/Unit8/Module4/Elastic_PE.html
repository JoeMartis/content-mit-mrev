Elastic Potential Energy

Potential Energy from a Spring
\( U^{e}(x) =\frac{1}{2}kx^2\)

where x is the displacement measured with respect to the equilibrium position (where the force is zero). The reference point: \(U(x=0) = 0\).

Calculate the elastic potential energy due to a spring of spring constant k. [show]
The work done by a horizontal spring of spring constant k on an object of mass m was obtained in the previous module:

\( W_{i,f} = -\frac{1}{2}(x_f^2 - x_i^2) \)

where \(x_f\) and \(x_i\) are the final and initial position of the object. The change in potential energy of the object is:

\(U_i(x_f) = U(x_i) -W_{i,f}\)

or

\( U(x_f) = U(x_i) + \frac{1}{2}k(x_{f}^2 - x_{i}^2)\;\)


Note:

When the object moves away from the equilibrium position the work done by the force of the spring is negative, and the kinetic energy of the object decreases. By the same account, the potential energy increases. There is a transfer of kinetic energy into potential.

Setting a reference point

Above we have obtained the change of potential energy, if we set a reference point for the potential energy then we can talk about the potential energy of the object. We set the reference of the potential energy to be zero at the equilibrium position: U(x = 0) = 0

Then the elastic potential energy of the object is:

\( U(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^2 \)
