Cause of change of Mechanical Energy

Forces that change the mechanical energy of the system

Work done by:

1. Non-conservative Forces change the mechanical energy of a system, and can arise from internal forces that add or subtract mechanical energy (e.g. an explosion or friction respectively) as well as from external forces. Work from such forces must be calculated explicitly.

2. External Conservative Forces arise from a conservative interaction between one object inside the system and another object outside the system. As a result, the work associated to it will change the mechanical energy of the system. Work from such forces must be calculated explicitly.

Cause of energy transformation between kinetic and potential

Work done by Internal Conservative Forces arise from conservative interactions that operate between two objects, both within the system. They can change kinetic energy into potential energy and vice versa, but they don't change the mechanical energy of the system. One need not calculate the work from conservative forces; it is taken into account by their Potential Energy.


Constant Mechanical Energy Model

When there is no work done by non-conservative and external forces the mechanical energy of the system is constant.

\( E_{f}^{mech} = E_{i}^{mech}\)

During the process that transformed the system from its initial to its final state there is an interchange of kinetic energy to potential and vice versa in such a way that the mechanical energy is kept constant.
