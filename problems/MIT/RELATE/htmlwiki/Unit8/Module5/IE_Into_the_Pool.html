Illustrative Example: Into the Pool

A child enters a swimming pool by walking slowly to the end of the diving board and falling vertically into the water. In the process, the child's center of mass drops 1.0 m vertically. Find the kinetic energy possessed by the child upon reaching the water.
Solution [show]
System: [show system]
The child, treated as a point particle, plus the earth treated as an infinitely massive object.

Interactions: [show interactions]
The earth plus child generate a gravitational interaction.

Model: [show Model]
Constant Mechanical Energy.

Approach: [show approach]

Note: By treating the Earth as an infinitely massive object we are neglecting the change of kinetic energy of the Earth while the child is jumping
Step 1: Draw an Initial State-Final State Diagram [show]
Much like in the momentum description of motion, it is useful to sketch the initial and final configuration of the system.

Step 2: Represent the Model Mathematically [show]
Our master equation for this section is:

\( K_{f} + U_{f} = K_{i} + U_{i}\;\)

After removing zeros, we have:

\( K_{f} = U_{i}\;\)

Giving:

\( K_{f} = mgy_{i} = (\mbox{25 kg})(\mbox{9.8 m/s}^{2})(\mbox{1.0 m}) = \mbox{250 J}\;\)
