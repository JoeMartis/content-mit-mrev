Illustrative Example: Two connected blocks pulled upward by ropes.

Two blocks connected by an ideal rope (A) are pulled upward by a second rope (B) attached to the upper block as shown in the figure below. The magnitude of the force of tension applied to block 2 is known and is equal to F2B. Because the blocks are connected by a rope of constant length (it does expand or become slack) they are constrained to move upward together with the same acceleration.

1. Concept Questions [show]
Question 1: What system would you use to calculate the acceleration of block 1? [answer]
Possible systems:
  • Combined block1+rope A+ block 2 system.
  • 3 separate systems: block 1, rope A and block 2.

The straightforward way to calculate the acceleration is to consider the combined system. The reason is that the force of tension due to the connecting rope A is not known and by considering the combined system it will be an internal force, therefore it will not contribute to the acceleration of the system.

Question 2: What system would you use to calculate the force of tension of rope A? [answer]
Possible systems:
  • Combined system first, then the system formed by block 1 only.
  • 3 separate systems: block 1, rope A and block 2.
  • Combined system first, then the system formed by block 2 only.

If we choose the combined (block 1+ rope A + block 2) system the force of tension is an internal force and we will not be able to get any information about it. Therefore you will have to use a system where the force of tension is an external force: either block 1 or block 2. The shortest solution is to use the combined system first to obtain the acceleration and then use block 1 as the system to obtain the tension.

2. Obtain the acceleration [show]

We will solve the problem using two approaches. First approach, the shortest, starts by considering the combined system formed by block 1+rope A+block 2. The second approach, the longest, starts by considering the three separate systems: block 1, rope A and block 2.

Combined system : [show]
  • System:block 1+rope A+block 2 is treated as a point particle of mass m1+m2.
  • Interactions: the system interacts with the Earth and Rope B.
  • The resulting forces of these interactions are shown in the free body diagram above, where the subscript (1+2) is used to indicate the point particle of mass m1+m2. The equation below the FBD is the y - component of Newton's second law written consistent with a coordinate system with the y - axis positive upwards. (There are no horizontal forces in this problem).


Three Separate systems : [show]
  • Systems:
    • System 1: block 1 assumed to be a point particle of mass m1.
    • System 2: rope A treated as a point particle of no mass, mA = 0.
    • System 3: block 2 treated as a point particle of mass m2.
  • Interactions: Block 1 interacts with the Earth and rope A. Rope A interacts with bock 1 and block 2 (no mass - no interaction with Earth). Block 3 interacts with rope A, rope B and the Earth. The resulting forces from these interactions are shown in the free body diagrams in the figure above.
  • The equations below the FBD's are Newton's Second Law for each of the systems written consistent with a coordinate system with the y - axis positive upwards.

Note that we have 3 independent equations and seven unknowns: 4 tension forces, the acceleration of the two blocks and the acceleration of the system. However, the tension forces are Newton's third law pairs (we have 2 equations more), and because the rope has a constant length it constraint the blocks to move with the same acceleration (2 extra equations).

In summary, to obtain the acceleration, add equations (1), (2) and (3) and consider:

  • Newton's third law constraint applied to the interaction between block 1 and rope A. As a result, the forces between block 1 and rope A have the same magnitude F1A = FA1. Their directions are already considered in the signs of the terms in equations (1) and (2).
  • Newton's third law constraint to the interaction between block 2 and rope A. As a result, the forces between block 2 and rope A have the same magnitude F2A = FA2. Their directions are already considered in the signs of the terms in equations (2) and (3).
  • Related accelerations constraint: the blocks must move together a1,y = a2,y = a, the acceleration of the system.
  • The connecting rope has zero mass: mA = 0
