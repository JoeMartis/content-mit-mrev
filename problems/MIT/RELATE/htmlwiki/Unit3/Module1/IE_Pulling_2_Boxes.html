Illustrative Examples: Pulling two boxes connected by a rope

Two boxes of masses m1 and m2 are connected by an ideal rope (Rope A in the figure). A second ideal rope, Rope B, is attached to the box of mass m2. A person with non-skid shoes is pulling Rope B with a horizontal force of magnitude F along a horizontal and smooth surface.

Given this situation you can think about different systems to analyze: one box, the two boxes together, the two boxes and the rope, the two boxes and the person all together, etc. The information you wish to obtain will suggest the best system to select. The choice of the proper system will be discussed in the following modules.


In this module we want to concentrate on identifying the interactions and classifying the resulting forces between the different objects that we have defined as system and the objects in the environment. For that purpose we will consider first a system containing only one box and then a system containing both boxes and the rope.

The procedure that we will use to analyze the systems and interactions is summarized below in 3 steps. These are the steps you should follow when you start solving almost all of the problems we will see in this course.

  1. Select the system (one or several objects) and by selecting the system you are defining the environment.
  2. Identify the interactions between the objects in the environment and in the system (external interactions). If there is more than one object in the system, identify the interactions between the objects inside the system (internal interactions).
  3. Classify the forces resulting from each of the interactions identified in steps 2 and 3 and draw the corresponding Free Body Diagrams.
