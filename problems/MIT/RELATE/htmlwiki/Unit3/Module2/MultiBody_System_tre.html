Multi-Body Systems Treated as a Single Point Particle

Newton's 3rd Law has a profound consequence when applied to a multi-body system. If we define the "average" position of the system in a special way (as the center of mass of the system) then Newton's 3rd Law guarantees that forces exerted on one member of the system by another system constituent will cancel and have no effect on the system's average position. Without this fact, the universe would be a far more complicated place. Even the simplest everyday objects are really systems composed of a trillion trillion atoms. If the interatomic forces had any impact on the motion of the object as a whole, it would require supercomputers to analyze the motion of a baseball.

Learning Goals

After completing this module, you should be able to:

  • Explain why a multi-body system can be treated as a point particle
  • Relate the internal forces and Newton's 3rd Law pairs
  • Explain why only the external forces are relevant to calculate the acceleration of the system
