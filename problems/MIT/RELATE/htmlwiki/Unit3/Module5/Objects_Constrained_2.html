Objects Constrained to Move with Different Accelerations

In this module we introduce ideal pulleys. An ideal pulley does not have mass and it can rotate without friction about its axis. In other words, no work is needed to rotate the pulley. The only purpose of the pulley is to change the direction of the rope and, with it, the direction of the force of tension acting on the different objects to which the rope is connected. As a result, these objects are constrained to move with different accelerations as shown in the illustrative examples in this module.

Learning Goals

After completing this module you should be able to:

  • Explain the need for using separate systems when considering the motion of a group of interacting objects constrained to move with different accelerations.
  • Express mathematically the relationship between the accelerations of the different objects connected by ropes through pulleys
  • Determine the accelerations of the objects connected by ropes through pulleys
  • Give a procedure for determining the associated forces of tension
