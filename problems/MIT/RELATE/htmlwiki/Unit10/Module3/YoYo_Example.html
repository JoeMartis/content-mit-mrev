Illustrative Example: The Yo-Yo

Consider a yo-yo, as shown in the picture.


Part A: Draw a torque diagram for the yo-yo as it moves downward. [show]
System: [show]
The yo-yo is treated as a rigid body.
Interactions: [show]
External influences from the earth (gravity) and the yo-yo's string (a contact force).
Diagramatic Representation [show]

The yo-yo can be represented as a set of cylinders: one smaller cylinder inside two larger ones. Gravity acts directly downwards at the center of mass. The force from the string acts upward at its point of contact with the inner cylinder. The net force is downward, and the net torque unwinds the yo-yo.
