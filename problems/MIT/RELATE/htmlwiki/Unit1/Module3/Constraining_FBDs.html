Constraining Free Body Diagrams using Newton's Laws

When constructing each free body diagram (FBD), think carefully about how the forces acting on the body must be consistent with Newton's Second and Third Laws:

  • For every force in the diagram, you should be able to identify both the type of interaction that causes it and the other object involved in this interaction.
  • Newton's Second Law allows you to spot missing forces because the Net Force on the body must be in the direction of the body's acceleration (or zero if the body does not accelerate). If all of the forces you have identified do not satisfy this Net Force condition, you can be certain you have missed some interaction.
  • Repeat: all the forces drawn in one FBD must be forces acting on the body in that diagram.

When considering two or more FBDs for interacting objects:

  • Newton's Third Law requires the sizes of the forces pairs to be equal (FAB = FBA) and the directions of these to be opposite. These forces are applied on the different objects. For example, FAB must appear in the FBD of object A, and FBA must appear in the FBD of object B.

Finally

  • Third Law force pairs are never present in the free body diagram for a single object. (Why?)
