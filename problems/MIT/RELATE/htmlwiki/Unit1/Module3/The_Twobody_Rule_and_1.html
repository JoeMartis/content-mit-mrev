Newton's Third Law

All interactions occur between two objects whose ensuing motion reflects the interaction. This "two-body rule" was codified by Newton in his Third Law of Motion.

Learning Goals

After completing this module you should be able to:

  • State Newton's Third Law.
  • Explain how Newton's Third Law implies that all interactions must involve two objects.
  • Use double subscript notation to indicate the pair of objects whose interaction produces a given force.

Statement of the Law

Newton's Third Law: The mutual interaction between two objects gives rise to equal and opposite forces on those two objects. We call this resulting pair of forces a third-law pair (sometimes it is referred to as an action-reaction pair). Thus, every force produced on a given body is always accompanied by an equal and opposite force exerted on the (different) body that is the interaction partner.
