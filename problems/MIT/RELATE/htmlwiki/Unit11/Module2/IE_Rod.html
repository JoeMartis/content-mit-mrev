Illustrative Example: Angular Momentum of a Rod

Consider a rod of length D with mass M distributed uniformly throughout it. The rod rotates within the x-y plane with an angular speed ω. Calculate the angular momentum about:

  1. the axis of symmetry of the rod (left figure)
  2. the axis perpendicular to the x-y plane passing through point Q (right figure).

Answer: [show]
1. About the axis of symmetry: Laxis = (MD2/12)ω.

2. About the axis passing through point Q: LQ = (MD2/3)ω.

Solution [show]
To answer question 1 we are given the moment of inertia about an axis that passes through point Q (right figure): Iaxis,Q = MD2/3 . We can obtain the moment of inertia about an axis that passes through the center of mass using the parallel axis theorem.:

Iaxis,Q = Icm + M(D / 2)2

Now we can solve for the moment of inertia about the center of mass of the rod:

Icm = Iaxis,Q − M(D / 2)2 = MD2 / 12

Because the axis that passes through the rod's center of mass is the axis of symmetry, the angular momentum about the axis of symmetry is:

Laxis,sym = (MD2 / 12)ω

and the angular momentum about the axis passing through point Q is:

Laxis,Q = (MD2 / 3)ω
