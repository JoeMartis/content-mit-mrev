Angular Momentum of Two Particles Rotating about a Fixed Axis

In the following examples you will see that:

  • For symmetric objects:
    • The angular momentum vector is parallel to the angular velocity vector
    • The angular momentum vector does not depend on the location of the point along the axis
    • \(\vec{L}_S = I_{axis}\;\vec{\omega}\) where S is any point along the axis and \(I_{axis}\) is the moment of inertia of the object about the axis of rotation.
  • For asymmetric objects:
    • The angular momentum vector is not parallel to the angular velocity vector

Two balls attached by a rod

Let's consider a rigid object consisting of two small balls of masses m1 and m2 connected by a rod of length 2R and negligible mass. The object is placed on a horizontal and frictionless table contained in the x-y plane. The object is set to rotate counterclockwise as viewed from above the x-y plane with an angular speed ω. The axis of rotation passes through the center of the rod, point Q in the left figure. The point S in the right figure is located in the axis of rotation at a distance d below the point Q.

Question: What is the direction and the magnitude of the angular momentum vector about point Q?
Check your answer [show]
Direction: +z axis, parallel to ω, Magnitude: LQ = (m1R2 + m2R2)ω
Solution [show]
The angular momentum of the system about point Q is the sum of the angular momentum of the constituents. There is no angular momentum associated to the rod because we consider it to be massless, then

\(\vec{L}_{Q} = \vec{L}_{Q}^{1} + \vec{L}_{Q}^{2}\)


Direction:

Consider the balls' position vectors measured from point Q to be \(\vec{r}_Q^1\) and \(\vec{r}_Q^2\), and the balls' velocity vectors to be \(\vec{v}_1\) and \(\vec{v}_2\) as shown in the figure:

Following the right hand rule, the direction of the angular momentum of each particle is along the +z axis. Adding both vectors, the total angular momentum about point Q is also along the +z axis parallel to ω.

Magnitude:

The angular momentum of ball 1 with respect to point Q is:

\(\vec{L}_{Q}^{1} = m_1\vec{r}_Q^1\times \vec{v}_{1} \)

and, the angular momentum of ball 2 with respect to point Q is:

\(\vec{L}_{Q}^{2} = m_2\vec{r}_Q^2\times \vec{v}_{2} \)

where, the position vectors of both balls measured from point Q have the same magnitude and equal to the radius of the circle:

\( |\vec{r}_Q^1| = |\vec{r}_Q^2| = R\)

Because the particles are connected by he rod they both have the same angular velocity of magnitude ω. As a result, their speeds are equal: v1 = v2 = Rω. The position and velocity vectors are perpendicular to each other, therefore the magnitude of the angular momentum vector of ball 1 is

\( L_{Q}^{1} = m_1|\vec{r}_Q^1| |\vec{v}_1|\sin 90^o = m_1Rv_1 = m_1R^2\omega\)

and for ball 2:

\( L_{Q}^{2} = m_2|\vec{r}_Q^2 ||\vec{v}_2|\sin 90^o = m_2Rv_2 = m_2R^2\omega\)


The total angular momentum about point Q is:

LQ = m1R2ω + m2R2ω = Iaxisω

where we have identified the terms multiplying ω as the moment of inertia of the two particles about the axis passing through point Q.

Note:

In this particular example, the angular momentum of each ball about point Q and consequently the angular momentum of the rigid object are parallel to the angular velocity:

\(\vec{L}_Q \parallel \vec{\omega}\)

Question 2: What is the direction and the magnitude of the total angular momentum about point S if both particles have the same mass, m1 = m2 = m?
Check your answer [show]
Direction:+z axis, Magnitude: LS = 2mR2ω
Solution: [show]
The angular momentum about point S of both balls are shown in the figure below. To obtain the total angular momentum we need to add both vectors.

Direction:

For each particle, the angular momentum about point S:

  • is not parallel to ω (see Question 2 of Example 2, "What's the point? of the previous module).
  • is at an angle \(\varphi\) with respect to the x-y plane. (This angle is the same for both because the particles are placed symmetrically with respect to the axis of rotation).
  • has a projection on the x-y plane (see figure).

If m1 = m2 = m then:

  • the magnitude of the angular momentum of each particle are equal.
  • the projection of the angular momentum vector on the x-y plane are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction.


 

Magnitude:

When adding the angular momentum of each particle the x-y plane components cancel and the total angular momentum is given by the sum of the z-components. As shown in Question 3 of Example 2, "What's the point? in the previous module, the magnitude of the angular momentum of each particle is:

\(L_S^1 = L_S^2 = (mR^2\omega)/\sin\varphi\)

The z-component is obtained by multiplying the above expression by \(\sin\varphi\):

\(L_{Sz}^1 = L_{Sz}^2 = mR^2\omega\)

The total angular momentum is along the z axis and has a magnitude of:

LS = 2mR2ω

Remarks:

From the results obtained in examples 1 and 2 we conclude that:

  • If \(m_1 \neq m_2 \Longrightarrow \vec{L}_Q \neq \vec{L}_S\) (the angular momentum vector depends on the point)
  • If \(m_1 \neq m_2 \Longrightarrow \vec{L}_S\) is not parallel to \(\vec{\omega}\), (where S is any point along the axis except the center of the circle).
  • If \(m_1 = m_2 \Longrightarrow \vec{L}_Q = \vec{L}_S = 2mR^2\; \vec{\omega}\).

When the masses are equal the angular momentum vector is independent of the location of the point S along the axis. In addition, it is parallel to the angular velocity vector:

\(\vec{L}_S \;=\;2mR^2\vec{\omega}\)

Note that the coefficient in the expression above is the moment of inertia of the rigid object about its axis of rotation: \(I_{axis}=2mR^2\):

For symmetric objects, object whose mass is distributed symmetrically about their axis of rotation, the angular momentum about any point along the axis is:

\(\vec{L}_S=I_{axis}\;\vec{\omega}\)
