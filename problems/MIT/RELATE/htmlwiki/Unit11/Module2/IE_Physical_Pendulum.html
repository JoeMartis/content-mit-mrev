Illustrative Example: A Physical Pendulum

Consider a uniform rod of mass M and length D. The rod rotates with an angular speed ω about an axis perpendicular to the xy-plane and that passes through one of its ends, point Q in the figure. A uniform disk of mass m and radius R is attached to the rod. The center of mass of the disk is at a distance d with respect to point Q. Calculate the angular momentum about the axis passing through point Q.

Answer: [show]
Laxis = ( MD2/3 + mR2/2 + md2 ) ω
Solution [show]
The angular momentum of a composite object is obtained as the sum of the angular momentum of each of its constituents:

\(L_{axis} = L_{axis}^{rod} + L_{axis}^{disk}\)

Because the rod and the disk are attached they move with the same angular speed ω, then:

\(L_{axis} = (I_{axis}^{rod} + I_{axis}^{disk}) \omega\)

For the rod:

Using the table of moments of inertia we find that the moment of inertia about the axis passing through point Q:

\(I_{axis}^{rod} = MD^2/3\)

For the disk:

In the table of moment of inertia we find the moment of inertia of the disk about its center of mass to be mR2/2. Using the parallel axis theorem we obtain the moment of inertia about he axis passing through point Q:

\(I_{axis}^{disk} = I_{axis}^{disk} + md^2 = mR^2/2 + md^2\)

Then, the angular momentum about the axis passing through point Q is:

Laxis = ( MD2/3 + mR2/2 + md2 ) ω
