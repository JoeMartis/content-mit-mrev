Illustrative Example: Angular Momentum of a Hoop

Consider a hoop of mass M and radius R contained in the xy-plane as shown below. The mass is distributed uniformly throughout the hoop.

If the hoop is rotating with angular velocity ω, calculate the angular momentum of the hoop:

  1. about the axis of symmetry (left figure),
  2. about an axis perpendicular to the xy-plane, passing through point Q (right figure).

Answer: [show]
1. About the axis of symmetry: Laxis = MR2ω

2. About the axis passing through point Q: LQ = 2MR2ω

Solution: [show]
Rotation about axis of symmetry:

We can think the hoop to be formed by a series of small elements of mass dm. In the figure below a pair of elements placed symmetrically with respect to the axis of rotation are shown.

The angular momentum of the two elements dm placed in diametrically opposite points in the hoop is the same as the one obtained for the two balls attached by a rod presented in the Examples 1 and 2, Angular Momentum of Two Particles in this module, where the angular momentum about the axis is found to be parallel to the angular velocity. To obtain the angular momentum of all the ring we will have to add the angular momentum of each of the element of masses. This sum becomes an integral:

\(L_{axis}=(\int dmr_{\perp}^2)\omega = I_{axis}\omega\)

where the the moment of inertia about the axis of rotation is Iaxis = MR2

About the axis passing through Q:

The angular momentum about the axis passing through Q:

LQ = Iaxisω

where the moment of inertia is obtained using the parallel axis theorem as Iaxis = 2MR2ω.
