Angular Momentum of a Rigid Body that is Both Rotating and Translating

Learning Goals

After working through this module, you should be able to:

  • calculate the angular momentum of a rigid object that translates and rotates about an axis that does not change direction during the object's motion.
  • explain that the angular momentum of an object that rotates and translates is the sum of two components: the orbital and the spin.

Introduction

In this course we will consider objects translating and rotating about an axis perpendicular to the plane where the objects move. Some examples are shown in the figure below:

During the complete motion of the objects in the examples above, the axis of rotation:

  • passes through the center of mass of the object (cm),
  • is perpendicular to the x-y plane,
  • translates following the path of the center of mass, and
  • does not change direction.


For a rigid body that is free to rotate and translate, the motion of the object can be thought of as a combination of translation of the center of mass and rotation about the center of mass. For a truly rigid body that cannot deform at all, the combination of translation of the center of mass plus spin about the center of mass is sufficient to reproduce any possible motion.
