Illustrative Example: Dropped Putty Wad

A putty wad of mass m is released from rest from an initial height h. A rod of mass M and length d is pivoted about its center (point Q in the figure). The rod is originally horizontal and at rest as shown. Right after the putty wad hits the right end of the rod, it sticks to it and both starts to rotate in the xy-plane about an axis passing through the pivot perpendicular to the xy-plane.

Question 1. Consider the collision process that takes place in a time interval ti < t < tf, where

  • ti is the instant right before the putty wad hits the right end of the rod at a speed vo, and
  • tf is the instant right after the putty wad sticks to the rod and both rotate together with angular speed ωo .

Is the angular momentum about point Q of the rod + putty wad system constant during the collision?

Answer [show]
Yes
Explanation [show]

System: Rod treated as a rigid object and the putty wad treated as a point particle.

External interactions:

Rod ⇐⇒ Earth. The resulting force is the weight of magnitude Mg along the -y-axis. The weight is applied at point Q, therefor there is no torque due to the weight at that point (zero moment arm).

Rod ⇐⇒ Axle. The resulting force is the force exerted by the axle passing through point Q on the rod. If the axle's diameter is small, then the torque about point Q is zero (zero moment arm).

Putty Wad ⇐⇒ Earth. The resulting force is the weight of magnitude mg along the -y-axis. The weight is applied at the putty wad which position is \(\vec{r}_1\) measured from point Q as shown in the figure below. Then the torque about point Q due to the weight is:

\[\vec{\tau}_Q^{mg} = \vec{r}_1\times m\vec{g}=-(dmg/2)\hat{k}\]

Internal Interactions:

Putty Wad ⇐⇒ Rod. The forces resulting from this interaction are the collision forces between the two objects. They constitute a Newton's 3rd law pair and they do not contribute to the net torque because their torques cancel each other.

The net torque about point Q is different from zero:

\(\sum\vec\tau_Q^{external}=\tau_Q^{mg} = -dmg/2\hat{k}\)


then


\[\frac{d\vec{L}_Q}{dt}^{system} = -dmg/2\hat{k}\]

implies that the angular momentum of the system is not constant during the collision.

Short time Collision Approximation:

Here we will give a similar argument as the one presented in the short time collision problems treated with linear momentum and impulse.

Integrating the above equation we obtain:

\[\vec{L}_Q^{system}(t_f) = \vec{L}_Q^{system}(t_i) +\int_{t_i}^{t_f}(-dmg/2\hat{k})dt\]


\[\vec{L}_Q^{system}(t_f) = \vec{L}_Q^{system}(t_i) - dmg/2(t_f - t_i)\hat{k}\]

If the time interval of collision, Δt = tf − ti, is very small then the second term in the right hand side of the equation, called angular impulse, is small compared to the first term and can be neglected. In this situation, we can say that the final angular momentum is approximately the same as the initial angular momentum.

Check:

We can compare the second and first term by doing the ratio of their magnitudes.

The initial angular momentum of the system is the angular momentum of the putty wad (the rod is at rest). This corresponds to the angular momentum of a point particle moving along a straight line a distance d/2 form point Q. The magnitude of the initial angular momentum of the system is then: \(L_Q^{system}=dmv_o/2\).

The ratio of the 2nd to the 1st term becomes:

\[\frac{dmg/2}{dmvo/2}\Delta t = \frac{g}{\sqrt{2gh}}\Delta t =\sqrt{g/2h}\Delta t \]

where, we have used the fact that the putty wad is released from rest from a height h, therefore the speed right before hitting the rod using the constant acceleration model is: \(v_o = \sqrt{2gh}\).

Now, let's give some reasonable numbers: h ~1 m Δ t ~ 10-3s

To obtain:

\[\frac{dmg/2}{dmvo/2}\Delta t = \sqrt{10/2}s^{-1}10^{-3}s \approx 0.002 \]

We can neglect the angular impulse due to gravity during the short time collision process.

Question 2. Consider a new process that last a time interval Δt = tf − ti, where now:

  • ti , is the instant right before the putty wad is released from rest from a height h, and
  • tf, is the instant right after the putty wad sticks to the rod an both start rotate together with an angular speed ωo.

Is the angular momentum about point Q of the rod + putty wad system constant during the process?

Answer [show]
No
Explanation [show]

The external interactions between the elements in the system and the environment are the same as those described in the explanation to the answer of question 1. The net external torque about point Q is the torque due to the gravitational force on the putty wad:

\[\sum\vec\tau_Q^{external}=\tau_Q^{mg} = -dmg/2\hat{k}\]

The difference now is that the torque due to gravity has enough time to change the angular momentum of the putty wad. The time of falling from a height h is obtained using the constant acceleration model: \( t = v_o/g =\sqrt{gh/2} = \sqrt{10.1/2} s \approx 2 s\)

The angular momentum of the system is not constant during this time interval.
