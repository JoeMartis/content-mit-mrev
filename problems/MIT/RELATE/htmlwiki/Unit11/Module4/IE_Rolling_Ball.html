Ball Rolling on a Horizontal Surface

A uniform ball of mass m and radius R is rolling on a horizontal surface as shown. The ball translates with a speed vcm and rotates with an angular speed ω about an axis passing through its center of mass parallel to the z-axis. The angular speed is large enough to make the ball slide with respect to the surface. Consider a point Q on the surface and a point S along the straight line followed by the ball's center of mass.

Question 1. Does the angular momentum of the ball about point Q remain constant during the ball's motion?

Answer [show]
Yes
Explanation [show]
System: ball treated as a rigid object.

Interactions:

Ball ⇐⇒ Earth. The resulting force is the weight along the -y-axis, applied at the center of mass. Its position about point Q is the vector \(\vec{r}_2\) in the figure.

Ball ⇐⇒Surface. The resulting force is the contact force applied at the point of contact between the two surface. Its position about point Q is the vector \(\vec{r}_1\) in the figure. The contact force has two components, the normal in the +y-direction and friction, in the -x-direction. Note: Because the ball is sliding relative to the surface, the force of friction is kinetic friction.

The sum of the torques due to the three forces on the ball about point Q is zero, then the angular momentum of the ball about that point is constant.

Question 2. Does the angular momentum of the ball about point S remain constant during the ball's motion?

Answer [show]
No
Explanation [show]
System: ball treated as a rigid object.

Interactions:

Ball ⇐⇒ Earth. The resulting force is the weight along the -y-axis, applied at the center of mass. Its position about point S is the vector \(\vec{r}_4\) in the figure.

Ball ⇐⇒Surface. The resulting force is the contact force applied at the point of contact between the two surface. Its position about point S is the vector \(\vec{r}_3\) in the figure. The contact force has two components, the normal in the +y-direction and friction, in the -x-direction. Note: Because the ball is sliding relative to the surface, the force of friction is kinetic friction.

The sum of the torques due to the three forces on the ball about point S is NOT zero then the angular momentum of the ball about that point is not constant.
