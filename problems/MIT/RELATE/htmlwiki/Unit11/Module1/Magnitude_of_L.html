The Magnitude of the Angular Momentum Vector

Following the definition of cross product, the magnitude of the angular momentum vector about point Q is given by:

\[|\vec{L}_Q|= m|\vec{r}_Q \times \vec{v}|=mr_Qv\sin\theta\]

where the angle θ is the angle between the position and the velocity vectors as shown in figures A and B. To obtain θ, draw a line along the position vector (dotted red line in the figures), the angle between the dotted line and the velocity (or momentum) vector is the angle θ.

In the examples shown in figures A the angle between the position and momentum vectors is smaller than 90o, in figures B the angle is between 90o and 180o.
