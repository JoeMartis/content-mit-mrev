Illustrative Examples: What's the Point?

The angular momentum vector depends on the position vector of the point particle, therefore it depends on the point from which the particle's position is measured. This is illustrated in the following examples.

Example 1. A particle moving along a straight line.

Question 1: What are the direction and the magnitude of the particle's angular momentum about point Q?
Check your answer: [show]
Direction: into the screen, Magnitude:LQ = mdv
Solution: [show]
Consider the particle's position vector rQ measured from point Q as shown in the figure below.

Direction:

Following the right hand rule, the direction of the angular momentum is along the -z -axis (into the screen). This corresponds to a clockwise circulation about point Q as viewed from above the xy - plane.

Magnitude:

At the instant shown in the figure, the magnitude of the angular momentum vector is given by:

\[|\vec{L}_Q| = mr_Qv\sin\theta\]

where θ is the angle between the velocity and position vectors. We will consider the supplementary angle β (θ + β = 180o) as shown in the figure:



Note:

  • The magnitude of the angular momentum depends on d, therefore it will have different values for different points in space. It depends on the specified point.
  • The distance d is the perpendicular distance: \( d = r_{\perp}\)
  • Because you know the perpendicular distance you do not need to know the angle θ or the magnitude of the position vector rQ. The product of these two quantities is given: rQ sin θ = d.
  • Also, θ and rQ depend on time but their product rQ sin θ = d is constant.

Example 2: Answers - A particle moving in a circle of radius R. [show]
Question 1: What are the direction and the magnitude of the particle's angular momentum about point Q?
Check your answer: [show]
Direction: +z-axis, Magnitude:LQ = mRv = mR2ω
Solution: [show]
Consider the particle's position vector rQ measured from point Q as shown in the figure below.

Direction:

Following the right hand rule, the direction of the angular momentum is along the +z -axis , parallel to the angular velocity.

Magnitude:

The magnitude of the angular momentum vector is given by:

\[|\vec{L}_Q| = mr_Qv\sin\theta\]

where θ is the angle between the velocity and position vectors, which is 90o. The position vector is along the radius and the velocity, which is tangent to the circle, is perpendicular to the position.

Note:

  • The term mR2 in the expression of the angular momentum is the moment of inertai measured from an axis passing through point Q: LQ = mR2ω = IQω
  • For any instant of time, the direction of the angular momentum vector about point Q is parallel to the angular velocity: \(\vec{L}_Q \parallel \vec{\omega}\)
