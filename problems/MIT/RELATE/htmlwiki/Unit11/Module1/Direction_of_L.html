The Direction of the Angular Momentum Vector

The angular momentum is a vector perpendicular to the position and the linear momentum vectors. This follows from its definition using the vector product just like the definition for torque.

\[\vec{L}_{Q} \perp \vec{r}_{Q},\; \; \vec{L}_{Q} \perp \vec{v}\]


Equivalently, angular momentum is perpendicular to the plane defined by both vectors in the vector product (shown as the x-y plane in the figures below), and the right hand rule determines the convention.

The RIGHT HAND RULE [show]
1. Place the fingers of your right hand along the direction of vector \(\vec{r}_Q\),
 
2. Curl them towards vector \(\vec{v}\),
 
3. Your thumb is now pointing in the direction of the angular momentum.

If the position vector and the velocity are contained in the x-y plane then the angular momentum is either along the +z axis or along the -z axis. These two situations are shown in the figures below. Practice the right hand rule to reproduce the direction of the angular momentum vector shown in the figures.

The angular momentum is pointing UP along the +z-axis
As viewed from above the xy plane, the angular momentum is pointing OUT of the screen.
The circulation of the particle about the point Q is counterclockwise.
The angular momentum is pointing Down along the -z-axis
As viewed from above the xy plane, the angular momentum is pointing INTO the screen.
