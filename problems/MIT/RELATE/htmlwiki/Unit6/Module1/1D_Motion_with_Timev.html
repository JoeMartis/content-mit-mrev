Introduction to General 1-D Motion with Arbitrary Net Force

Thhe most general model applicable to a single particle moving in one dimension occurs when the driving force along this coordinate can be any function of time. This model forms the top of the 1-D motion hierarchy of models; 1-D motion with constant acceleration with constant force is a special case, and 1-D motion with constant velocity and zero net force is a special case of that. (These three models were presented to you in reverse order for ease of learning).

Learning Goals

  • Recognize when a motion diagram or a graph or equation of position or velocity vs. time, represents an object moving with non-constant (arbitrary) acceleration.
  • Describe whether the object represented in these ways has positive or negative acceleration and velocity..
  • Know how to create a graph or equation of any one of the three kinematic variables (position, velocity, acceleration) from a graph or equation of any other one (and possibly some initial conditions).

Model: Arbitrary Net Force in One Dimension

Applicable Systems

A point particle or the center of mass of a rigid body.

Relevant Interactions

Time-varying net force must be present to cause motion with variable acceleration along that one coordinate.

Laws of Change

For motion with arbitrary acceleration \(a(t)\).

Differential Form

Aligning the one dimension of motion with the x-coordinate, the differential forms of the Laws of Change are:

\[a_x(t)=\frac{dv_x}{dt}=\frac{1}{m}F_x(t)\mbox{\ and\ }v_x(t)=\frac{dx}{dt}\]

Integral Form

Integrating requires initial conditions for the velocity and position, denoted by \(v_i=v(t_i)\) and \(x_i=x(t_i)\):

\[v_x(t) = v_{i}+ \int_{t_i}^{t}a_x(\tau)d\tau \]

\[x(t) = x_{i}+ \int_{t_i}^{t}v_x(\tau)d\tau \]

Cues

  • If any of the kinematic variables (position, velocity, acceleration) has a given analytic expression, for example if \(v(t)=\alpha+\beta t+\gamma t^2\).
  • If a curve of any of the kinematic variables is given, graphical methods for integration and differentiation can be used to find the others.
