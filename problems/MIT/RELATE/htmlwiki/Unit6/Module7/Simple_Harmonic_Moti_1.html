Simple Harmonic Motion

In this module we explore the type of interaction that leads to sinusoidal oscillatory motion.

Learning Goals

After completing this module, you should be able to:

  • Recognize the physical conditions that cause Simple Harmonic Motion.
  • Find the angular frequency from the force law for any Simple Harmonic Motion.
  • Know the general solution for Simple Harmonic Motion and how to match given initial or final conditions.

Sinusoidal Position vs. Time

We will again specify a motion using position, then determine the acceleration and therefore the net force that must be present to cause this motion. This is the same approach we took with Uniform Circular Motion. Simple harmonic motion (also called simple harmonic oscillation) is defined to be one-dimensional motion such that the position is a sinusoidal function of time (a sine function and/or cosine function, both at the same frequency). This motion is by definition periodic (it repeats itself at regular intervals with period T).
