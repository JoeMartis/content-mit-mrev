Mechanical Energy in SHM

Learning Goals

  • Apply conservation of mechanical energy to the oscillations of a mass on a spring.
  • Give an example of a problem involving a mass on a spring that is more easily solved by using mechanical energy conservation than the Simple Harmonic Motion Model, and vice versa.
One of the most useful features of a simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) is the conservation of mechanical energy. When a good oscillator is set in motion, it will continue for a very long time unless interrupted. (We shall see later, however, that this can also be an annoying feature in certain real world applications.) The conservation of mechanical energy in a simple harmonic motion (SHM) can be a useful tool in problem solving as well. The equations of motion for a Simple Harmonic Oscillator that were outlined in the Simple Harmonic Motion (Linear Restoring Acceleration) Model are sufficiently simple that any problem in SHM can be solved using that Model alone, but knowing that the mechanical energy is conserved can sometimes greatly streamline a solution..
