Simple Harmonic Oscillation

Simple Harmonic Motion or "SHM" is a standard topic in introductory mechanics because it is a powerful illustration of how Models are applied in real-world problem solving. In this unit, we will use SHM to explore two important aspects of real world problem solving. First, we will use the fact that SHM can be described by two of the fundamental Models of the course to explore the process of choosing the most efficient description of a motion for a specific problem statement. Second, we will use the wide applicability of SHM to illustrate the concept of a fundamental mathematical form for a physical process.

Which Model is most Efficient?

We have already discussed the fact that an elastic restoring interaction is the prototypical source of linear restoring acceleration giving rise to simple harmonic motion. We have also shown that the elastic restoring interaction is conservative. Thus, if you are examining the motion of an object subject only to an elastic restoring interaction, you have the choice of using the Simple Harmonic Motion Model or the Constant Mechanical Energy Model. In this Unit, we will see that the Simple Harmonic Motion Model will always lead to a solution of any problem involving SHM, but using conservation of mechanical energy can sometimes be a more efficient path.

Same Differential Equation Equals Same Solution

Simple Harmonic Motion is a Model that is very widely used in real world applications. This is because a huge variety of physical phenomena can be approximately described by the Model under certain conditions. This approximate description will sometimes necessitate using a different quantity (often, but not necessarily, angle) in the role of position in the model. Understanding quantitatively when a specific Model can be approximately applied is a fundamental part of problem solving expertise. In this unit we will use SHM to illustrate the components of this understanding.
