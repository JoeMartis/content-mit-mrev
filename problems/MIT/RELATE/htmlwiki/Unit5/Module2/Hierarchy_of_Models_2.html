Hierarchy of Core Models with Particular Models

The five core models differ in their applicable systems, in the physical quantities that change, and in the types of interactions and forces that cause the change. For several of these core models, it is worth developing specialized models for particular cases. The case in which the agent of change is absent or zero is an especially important one for core models involving the fundamental physical quantities momentum, angular momentum, and mechanical energy because in this case the physical quantity remains constant (or "conserved").
