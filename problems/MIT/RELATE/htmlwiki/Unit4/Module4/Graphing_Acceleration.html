Position vs. Time Graphs and Acceleration

The concavity (or equivalently, the second derivative) of a position versus time graph can be used to determine the sign of the acceleration. A concave (viewed from positive y) position versus time graph has positive acceleration. The reason can be seen by considering the case of a system with constant positive acceleration. The position versus time graph for such a system will be an upward-opening parabola like that shown below.

The vertex of this parabola is a point where the slope of the graph goes to zero (at the bottom). A point of zero slope in a position vs. time graph implies that the velocity goes to zero at that time. Thus, the system is momentarily at rest at the time corresponding to the vertex of the parabola. Everywhere to the right of the vertex in the graph, the slope of the parabola is positive and increasing as time increases. Thus, the velocity is increasing in the positive direction, implying positive acceleration. Everywhere to the left of the vertex, the velocity is negative and approaching zero (becoming smaller in magnitude as time increases). This lessening of a negative velocity also corresponds to positive acceleration.
