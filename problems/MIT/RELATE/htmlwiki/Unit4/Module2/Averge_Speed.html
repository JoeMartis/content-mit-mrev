Average Speed

Suppose that you know only that an object starts from position xi at time t = ti and has moved to x(t) at time t. In many cases exact details of this motion don’t matter. In this example there may be a hill in the intervening block, but all we’re really interested in is, for example, that it took 2 minutes to go two blocks. Under these circumstances, we’re interested in the "average speed''. This is defined as

\(v_{avg}[ti, t] = \frac{x(t)-x_{i}}{t-t_{i}}\), and may be thought of as

\(v_{avg} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}=\frac{displacement}{interval}\).

The notation vavg[ti,t] emphasizes that the average velocity is a function of the particular time interval over which the motion occurs rather than any particular instant of time. In this case the average velocity is +1 block per minute. The "+" sign indicates the direction of the displacement; in this case the positive x direction is east.
