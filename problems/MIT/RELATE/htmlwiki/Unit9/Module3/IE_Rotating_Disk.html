Illustrative Example: Rotating Disk

A disk is free to rotate about an axle that passes through its center as shown below. A wire wraps around the disk, and is pulled by a force of magnitude F = 10 N. The radius of the disk is r = 0.20 m and the moment of inertia about the axis that passes through its center is 0.10 kg.m2.

What is the angular acceleration of the disk?

Answer [show]
System: [show]
The disk is treated as a rigid body
Interactions: [show]
The disk interacts with the wire (Force F), the Earth (gravity), and the axle (Normal force N: the contact force exerted by the axle on the disk, there is no friction).
Diagramatic Representation [show]
The forces acting on the disk are shown below:

Consider the coordinate system x-y in the plane of the disk as shown. For this coordinate system, a clockwise rotation as viewed from above the +z axis is negative.

We choose our axis of rotation to be the axle and we calculate torques about the point Q in the figure which is the point of intersection between the axis and the disk.

Mathematical Representation [show]
For the disk, we write Newton's 2nd Law :

\(\displaystyle \sum \tau_Q = I_Q \alpha \)

The force of gravity is applied at the center of mass of the disk (point Q), then it does not contribute to the total torque. The normal force exerted by the axle on the disk is applied at the point of contact between the axle and the disk. If the axle has a radius r1, (r1 < r), then the torque due to the normal force is \(\vec{\tau}_Q^N=\vec{r}_1\times\vec{N} = 0\). This torque is zero because r1 is parallel to N. The only torque that is not zero is the one due to the force F , which has a moment arm equal to the radius r, therefore:

\( \displaystyle \sum \tau_Q = I_Q \alpha \Longrightarrow -Fr = I_Q \alpha \)

After applying the right hand rule, the direction of the torque due to the force F is into the screen. According to our choice of coordinate system it is a negative torque.

Now we can find \( \displaystyle \alpha\)


\( \displaystyle \alpha= \frac{-Fr}{I_Q} = \frac{-10 \times 0.02}{0.1} = -2 \frac{rad}{sec}\)

The negative sign implies that the angular acceleration is pointing into the screen, along the -z axis. This is expected because the torque due to the force F (the net torque) is into the screen.
The disk is rotating clockwise as viewed from above the +z axis. This corresponds to an angular velocity pointing into the screen, in the same direction as the angular acceleration. The disk is speeding up.
