Illustrative Example: Moment of Inertia of Two Point Masses

A stick is fixed to rotate around a frictionless axle, and it has two beads attached to it at the ends.

Assume that the stick is massless, and that the small red masses are very small in size, and have mass = 0.050 kg. The masses are 15 cm from the center of rotation. What is the moment of inertia of this configuration?

Answer: [show]

In this case moment of inertia is

\( \displaystyle I= M R^2 + M R^2\)

\( I= 2 \times (0.050 kg) \times (0.15)^2 = 0.00225 \; kg \cdot m^2 \)
