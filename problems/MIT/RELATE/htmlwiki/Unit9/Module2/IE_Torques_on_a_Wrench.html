Illustrative Example: Torques on a Wrench

In the figure, a wrench is used to tighten or loosen a bolt. Calculate the torque about an axis passing through the center of the bolt due to each of the forces shown in the figure. The distance between the center of the bolt and the end of the wrench is 2d. Force F1 is applied at the end of the wrench, and forces F2 and F3 are applied at a distance d from the center of the bolt. Express your answer in terms of the magnitude of the forces, the distance d and the appropriate trigonometric functions.


Solution [show]
We consider the system to be the bolt and the wrench. The torque associated with each of the forces will cause the wrench-bolt system to rotate about an axis passing through the center of the bolt. Having identify the axis of rotation we proceed to identify:
  1. the points at which the forces are being applied
  2. the position vector of the point of application of each force measured from the axis
  3. the angles between each of the forces and the position vectors
