Illustrative Example: Torques on a Rod

In the following examples a force of magnitude F is applied at the end of a rod that is free to rotate about a vertical axis perpendicular to the x-y plane. The rod is initially at rest. Both the rod and the applied force are contained in the x-y plane. Consider the screen to be parallel to the x-y plane, therefore the +z axis is pointing out of the screen.

For each of the situations shown below, use the right hand rule to obtain the direction of the torque vector about the axis. Your answer should indicate if the torque is directed along the +z axis or along the -z axis.

To calculate the torque about the axis we need to consider the position of the point at which the force is applied measured from the axis. In the figures below, the position vector is the blue arrow and has magnitude r, its tail is at the red dot which is the point of intersection between the axis and the xy-plane, and its head is at point A, the point at which the force is applied. (For clarity we have not included the rod in the figures),

Now, follow the steps of the right hand rule to obtain the direction of the torque. Start by placing the fingers of the right hand along the position vector, the blue arrow. Then, curl them towards F. Your thumb must point in the direction of the torque shown in the second column of the table below. In the third column we included the resulting sense of rotation of the rod as a result of applying the torque due to the force F assuming that the rod is initially at rest.
