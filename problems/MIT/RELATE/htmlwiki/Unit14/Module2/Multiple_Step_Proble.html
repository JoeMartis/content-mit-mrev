In a multiple step problem the interactions that produce the system to change are not the same during the time interval it takes the system to go from its initial to its final state. This time interval has to be split into smaller time intervals during which the interactions do not change. As a result, the problem is decomposed into consecutive sub-problems.

In the figure above, the time interval ti < t < tf is divided into 4 sub-intervals, then it is necessary to solve 4 sub-problems. Each of the sub problems are solved by applying one or more models and by matching the final and initial states of the consecutive sub problems. For example, the final speed of a given object in step 2 is the initial speed of the object in step 3.

In kinematics this procedure is referred as motion decomposition (see example Fan-Powered Ice Boat). In the illustrative example below, the problem is decomposed into two sub problems: a collision followed by the compression of a spring.
