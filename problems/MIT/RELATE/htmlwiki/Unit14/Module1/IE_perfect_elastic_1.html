A perfect elastic collision.

In a perfect elastic collision, the forces resulting from the interaction between the two objects are conservative. As a result, the mechanical energy of the system consisting of the two objects is constant during the collision.

Right after the two objects start to be in contact, their shapes begin to deform and their kinetic energy before impact is stored into elastic potential energy. When the objects start to recover their original shape, they push away from each other and at the instant right when they loose contact, their original shape is completely restored and all the potential energy is transformed back to kinetic energy. Therefore, the kinetic energy of the system right before the collision is the same as the kinetic energy right after the collision.

Answer: [show]
No. Each time the ball bounces, it deforms and loses energy during the collision. As a result it is unable to regain its previous heights.
