Illustrative Example

Two identical boxes of mass m are placed in such a way to compress a spring a distance d from its equilibrium position. The spring is massless, has a spring constant k, and it is not attached to block 1. A rope is used to keep the blocks together. Both blocks are initially at rest. The rope is cut. Find the speed of box 1, v1, right at the instant when it loses contact with the spring.
ti : instant right after cutting the rope tf : instant right after 1 looses contact with the spring

We will consider the time interval ti < t < tf which corresponds to the time it takes the spring to expand from its initial compression of d until it pushes both boxes away loosing contact with box 1.

Looking at the system and interactions (SHOW)
System and Interactions:

System: The two boxes, treated as point particles, and the massless spring.

Interactions:

External:

  • The boxes and the Earth (the spring is massless so it does not interact with the Earth): gravitational force on both boxes.
  • The boxes and the frictionless surface: normal force on both boxes.

These external forces cancel each other:

  • \(\sum_{ext}\vec{F} = 0\)

and, the work done by the external forces is zero

  • \(W_{i,f}^{ext} = 0\)

Internal:

  • The spring interacting with Box1 and Box 2. The results of these interactions is a conservative force that can be represented as elastic potential energy.
we conclude that during this time interval the external and internal forces of the 2 boxes + spring system are such that:
1. Net External force 2. External work 3. Internal forces:
\(\sum_{ext}\vec{F} = 0\) \(W_{i,f}^{ext} = 0\) CONSERVATIVE

Then for ti < t < tf both the linear momentum (1) and the mechanical energy (2 and 3) of the 2 boxes + spring system are constant.

This problem is an example of a one step problem that is solved by applying two models simultaneously: constant momentum and constant mechanical energy.


Solution [show]
We will consider a coordinate system with the x-axis parallel to the surface and positive to the right:

1. Constant Linear momentum apply to the 2 boxes + spring system:

Initial momentum:

\(\vec{p}(t_i)= 0\)

Final momentum:

\(\vec{p}(t_f)= -mv_1\hat{i} + mv_2\hat{i}\)

Because it is constant:

\(\;\;\; \vec{p}(t_f)= \vec{p}(t_i)\)

\(-m\; v_1 + m\;v_2 = 0\;\;\;\;\; \mbox{(eq. 1)}\)

2. Constant Mechanical Energy apply to the 2 boxes + spring system.

Initial mechanical energy:

\(E(t_i) = \frac{1}{2}kd^2\)

Final mechanical energy:

\(E(t_f) = \frac{1}{2}mv_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}mv_2^2\)

Because it is constant:

\( E(t_f) = \;E(t_i) \)

\(\frac{1}{2}mv_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}mv_2^2 = \frac{1}{2}kd^2 \;\;\;\;\;\; \mbox{(eq. 2)}\)

From (eq. 1) we obtain that the speed of both boxes are the same. Replacing this result in (eq. 2), then:

\(\frac{1}{2}mv_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}mv_2^2 =\frac{1}{2}2mv_1^2 = mv_1^2= \frac{1}{2}kd^2\)

\(v_1 =\sqrt{\frac{k}{2m}}d\)

Note: In this example the mechanical energy is constant but the kinetic energy of the system increases, initially was zero and finally it is not. The source of the kinetic energy is the potential energy stored initially in the compressed spring.
