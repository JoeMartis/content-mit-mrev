In a one step problem the interactions that produce change in the system are the same during the time interval it takes the system to go from its initial to its final state.

The simplest of these type of problems are those that are solved by applying one single model. For example, calculate the speed of a given object at the bottom of a smooth incline after the object has been released from rest from the top of the incline. Using constant mechanical energy model we can obtain the answer.

In this module we will illustrate one step problems that are solved by applying, during the time interval under consideration, more than one model simultaneously. For example, in the problem below, the state of the system formed by the two boxes and the spring changes under the action of internal elastic forces. As a result, the constant momentum and the constant energy models are used simultaneously. Another example that you have already seen several times is the one of projectile motion. While the object is in air, it interacts with Earth only. As a result, the problems are solved by using the constant acceleration and the constant velocity models simultaneously.
