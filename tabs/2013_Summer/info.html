Grading

There are three types of problem: Checkpoint, Homework, and Quiz.

  • Checkpoint problems are mixed in with the text. Checkpoints are worth 20% of your total grade. They are graded on an "attempt" basis. You do not have to get them right, but you do have to try them.
  • Homework problems are found at the end of each unit. Homework is worth 40% of your total grade. You need to get 15 points total each time homework is due. (Typically this means 15 points per unit, but sometimes two units are due due together.) Homework problems only add to your total when you get them right.
  • Quizzes are worth 40% of your total grade. Each quiz problem is worth the same number of points. You must achieve a quiz grade greater than 60% to receive a certificate. The lowest quiz score is dropped.

    • Each problem - that is, each time you see the "Check/Save/Show Answer" boxes - is worth a number of points as indicated. Problems without a point total marked are worth 1 point. Most problems are worth between 1 and 3 points though some sections of multi-part problems are worth less.

    Points are only compared within a particular section, so homework points and quiz points should not be compared to each other. They are averaged separately before being combined into your grade.
