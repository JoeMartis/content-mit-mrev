The general physical models commonly taught in introductory mechanics are:

Models Describing Linear Motion (Force is Fundamental Description of Interactions)

  • Dynamics and Net Force
    • Two-Dimensional Motion
      • Uniform Circular Motion (Centripetal Acceleration)
      • One-Dimensional Motion
        • One-Dimensional Motion with Constant Acceleration
          • Constant Velocity (Zero Acceleration)
        • Simple Harmonic Motion (Linear Restoring Acceleration)

 

  • Momentum and External Impulse
    • Constant Momentum (Zero External Impulse)

 

Models Describing Angular Motion (Torque is Fundamental Description of Interactions)

  • Angular Velocity and Angular Acceleration about a Single Axis
    • Angular Motion about a Single Axis with Constant Angular Acceleration

 

  • Angular Momentum and External Angular Impulse about a Single Axis
    • Constant Angular Momentum (Zero External Angular Impulse)

 

Models Describing both Linear and Angular Motion (Force and Torque each used to calculate Work)

  • Mechanical Energy, External Work, and Internal Non-Conservative Work
    • Constant Mechanical Energy (Zero Non-Conservative Work)
