The gravitational interaction between the earth and an object near the earth's surface overwhelms the other gravitational interactions experienced by the object (in the reference frame of the earth). The gravitational force exerted on an object near the earth's surface is called the object's weight.

The Force of Gravity Near the Earth's Surface

Form of the Force

The force acting on an object of mass m near the earth's surface due to the gravitational interaction with the earth has the following properties:

  • The force points straight down (into the surface of the earth).
  • The force has magnitude mg where g = 9.8 m/s2 when mass is in kilograms.

Relationship to Universal Gravitation

The form of this force is a special case of Newton's law of universal gravitation. Objects on the earth's surface have negligible size (relative to the size of the earth) so they can be treated as point particles. Since the earth is essentially spherical, an object located anywhere on its surface experiences the same gravitational force interaction by the law of universal gravitation. For objects near the earth's surface, the separation between the object and the position of the earth's center is essentially equal to the radius of the earth, Re. Assuming that the earth has mass Me, the law of universal gravitation gives:

$ |F^{G}_{me}| = G \frac{mM_{e}}{R_{e}^{2}} $

which can be written:

$ |F^{G}_{me}| = m \left(G\frac{M_{e}}{R_{e}^{2}}\right)$

The expression in parenthesis is essentially constant, so that this expression has the form

$ |F^{G}_{me}| = mg $

if g is equal to:

$ g = G\frac{M_{e}}{R_{e}^{2}}$

Looking up the mass and radius of the earth gives:

$ g = G\frac{M_{e}}{R_{e}^{2}} = $
$ g = (6.67\times 10^{-11}\mbox{ N m}^{2}/\mbox{kg}^{2})\left(\frac{5.98\times 10^{24}\mbox{ kg}}{(6.37\times 10^{6}\mbox{ m})^{2}}\right) = $
g = $\mbox{9.83 m/s}^{2} $

Acceleration of Gravity

The form of the weight force is particularly simple. If it is the only force acting on an object, then Newton's Second Law says that:

$ F_{net} = mg = ma\; $

so that:

$ g = a \;$

Thus, for any object near the surface of the earth with net force equal to its weight, the acceleration will be 9.8 m/s2 downward.

Gravitational Potential Energy Near Earth's Surface

For a system consisting of the earth plus an object of mass m near the earth's surface, the gravitational potential energy will be a function of the height of the object, since the force of gravity is perfectly vertical. Taking y to be the vertical coordinate (positive upward, origin at an arbitrary height), and choosing the zero of the potential energy to lie at y = 0:

$ U^{g}_{me}(y=0) = 0 $

the potential energy will take the form:

$ U^{g}_{me}(y) = mgy$

This curve has no minimum. Thus, there is no position of equilibrium for an object moving under the influence of its weight alone. At least one other force must be present to produce equilibrium.
