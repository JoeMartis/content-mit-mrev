Restrictions to the Angular Momentum and External Angular Impulse about a Single Axis Model

Constant Angular Momentum is a sub-model of the Momentum and External Impulse Model. This model applies in the case that the net external torque applied to the system is zero, so that the system experiences no angular impulse.

Importance of Choice of Axis

Because the torque exerted by an external force depends on the choice of axis, it is sometimes possible to choose the axis of rotation to give zero net external torque even when the net external force is nonzero.

Approximate Application in Collisions

When describing the effects of a process that has an extremely short duration such as a collision it is often reasonable to neglect the external angular impulse, provided that the system is taken to encompass all the objects participating in the collision. During the collision, the contact forces exerted by the colliding objects upon each other will generally be much larger than any other forces acting on the objects participating in the collision. Thus, as long as the collision forces are defined to be internal, the external forces can generally be neglected.
