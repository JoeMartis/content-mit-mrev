Compatible Systems

This Model can describe the translational motion of a system composed of any number of point particles. Rigid bodies can be a part of the system, but must be treated as point particles located at their centers of mass.

Relevant Interactions

Only external forces change a system's momentum, since the effects of internal forces cancel by Newton's Third Law.

Definitions

Net External Impulse

$\vec{J}_{net} = \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} \sum_{ext} \vec{F}(t) dt$

Mathematical Representation

Differential Form (Newton's Second Law)

$\frac{d\vec{p}}{dt} = \sum_{ext} \vec{F}$

Integral Form

$\vec{p}_{f} = \vec{p}_{i} + \vec{J}_{net}$
