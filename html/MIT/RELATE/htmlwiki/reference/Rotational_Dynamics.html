Compatible Systems

This Model can only describe the rotational motion of a single rigid body executing pure rotation about a single axis of rotation. The axis of rotation must either be fixed in an inertial frame of reference or else must pass through the center of mass of the rigid body.

Relevant Interactions

The angular equivalent of Newton's Second Law combined with the cancellation of internal torques by Newton's Third Law implies that the angular acceleration of the system is equal to the net external torque divided by the moment of inertia of the rigid body about the axis of rotation. Thus, only external torques need be considered.

Definitions

Angular Acceleration about Axis a (Angular Equivalent of Newton's Second Law)

$\alpha_{a} = \frac{1}{I_{a}} \sum_{ext} \tau_{a} $

Mathematical Representation

Differential Form

$\frac{d\omega_{a}}{dt} = \alpha_{a}(t)$

$\frac{d\theta_{a}}{dt} = \omega_{a}(t)$

Integral Form

$\omega_{f,a} = \omega_{i,a} + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} \alpha_{a}(t) dt$

$\theta_{f,a} = \theta_{i,a} + \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} \omega_{a}(t) dt$
