Compatible Systems

This Model can simultaneously describe both the rotational and translational motion of a system composed of any number ofrigid bodies and point particles.

Relevant Interactions

Internal conservative interactions such as gravity and elastic restoring interactions are generally described by their associated potential energy, and therefore do not change the total mechanical energy of the system. External conservative interactions and any non-conservative interactions (internal or external) that perform work do change the total mechanical energy.

Definitions

Translational Kinetic Energy

$K = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}$

Rotational Kinetic Energy

$K_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^{2}$

Gravitational Potential Energy

Near Earth Gravity

$\mathcal{}U_{g} = mgh$


Universal Gravitation

$U_{g} = -\frac{Gm_{1}m_{2}}{r_{21}}$

Elastic Potential Energy

$U_{e} = \frac{1}{2}kx^{2}$

System Mechanical Energy

$\mathcal{ }E=\sum_{int}K+\sum_{int}K_{rot}+\sum_{int}U_{g}+\sum_{int} U_{e}$

Net Work

$ W_{net} = \int_{i}^{f} ( \sum_{ext\;{\rm or}\;NC} \vec{F}\cdot d\vec{r}\;)+ \sum_{ext\;{\rm or}\;NC} \vec{\tau}\cdot d\vec{\theta}) $

$W_{net}= \int_{t_{i}}^{t_{f}} (\sum_{ext\;\;{\rm or}\;\;NC} \vec{F}\cdot\vec{v}\;+ \sum_{ext\;\;{\rm or}\;\;NC} \vec{\tau}\cdot \vec{\omega}) dt$

Mathematical Representation

Differential Form

$\frac{dE}{dt} = \sum_{ext\;\;{\rm or}\;\;NC}\vec{F}\cdot \vec{v}\;+ \sum_{ext\;\;{\rm or}\;\;NC}\vec{\tau}\cdot \vec{\omega}$

Integral Form

$\mathcal{}E_{f} = E_{i} + W_{net}$
